## Universal Hook Taxonomy

Before picking a hook pattern, choose the motivational angle:

| Hook Type       | Formula                             | Nigerian Example                                                        |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Curiosity**   | "I was wrong about [X] until..."    | "I was wrong about wigs. I thought they all shed."                      |
| **Story**       | "Last [week/month], [someone]..."   | "Last Saturday, a customer messaged: 'my skin hasn't looked this good'" |
| **Value**       | "How to [outcome] without [cost]"   | "How to fade dark spots without harsh chemicals"                        |
| **Contrarian**  | "Unpopular opinion: [true thing]"   | "Unpopular opinion: expensive skincare ≠ better results."               |
| **Urgency**     | "[Number] [people] already [did X]" | "Over 200 Lagos women reordering this every month."                     |
| **Problem**     | "[Pain]? [Empathy statement]."      | "Dark spots, uneven tone? Lagos skin deserves better."                  |

**Use different hook types across variations** — never write two variations with the same hook type.

### JTBD Framing
People don't buy products. They hire them to do a job.

| Product        | What they're actually hiring it for                                 |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Skincare serum | "Fade dark spots before a big event without harsh chemicals"        |
| Wig            | "Look camera-ready at owambe without 3 hours in a salon"           |
| Nail service   | "Walk into Monday looking polished without DIY disasters"           |

Frame at least ONE copy variation around the job, not the product.

## BEAUTY
_Applies to: skincare, serum, cream, glow, makeup, lash, nail, hair care, natural hair, spa, waxing, organic beauty_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (visible result/identity) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT → LINE 4 PROOF → LINE 5 CTA

### Hook Patterns
- skincare: "The glow your skin has been asking for 💆‍♀️" / "In 4 weeks, your friends will ask what changed" / "Hyperpigmentation, dark spots — let's fix that"
- makeup: "The foundation that matches your skin, not theirs" / "Built for melanin. Finally."
- lash/nail: "Lashes that make people ask 'are those real?'" / "Nails done right — in [loc], by appointment"
- hair products: "Your natural hair IS growing. Wrong products." / "Length retention starts here 🌿"
- organic: "No chemicals. No compromise on results."

### Benefit Lines
- skincare: "Brightening serum for melanin-rich skin — non-greasy, absorbs fast, Nigerian weather tested"
- makeup: "Full coverage — no cracking, no ashiness, sweat-resistant for Lagos heat. Shades from deep brown to ebony."
- lash/nail: service details + appointment flow
- hair: "Seals moisture, prevents breakage — type 4a/4b/4c tested"

### Proof
✅ "Trusted by Lagos women with melanin-rich skin" / "Customers reorder every month" / "Formula built for African skin"
❌ HARD BLOCKS: "lightens skin", "clinically proven" (no citation), "cures acne", "guaranteed to grow", "before/after framing"
❌ Bleaching language → reframe ONLY as: "even tone", "fade dark spots", "brightening"

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "Message us — we'll match you to the right product for your skin type"
- Booking: "Book your appointment — slots fill every week"
- Learn more: "Tap to learn more about our products and services."

### WhatsApp Prefill
- skincare: "Hi! I saw your [serum/cream] ad. I have [concern]. What do you recommend and how much?"
- lash/nail: "Hi! I saw your [service] ad in [loc]. Availability this week and price for [service]?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Skincare Built for Nigerian Skin" | "Your Glow Starts Here"
- Urgency: "Why Lagos Women Keep Reordering This" | "Book Before This Week Fills Up"

### Psychology Layer
- **Identity anchoring**: "Built for melanin" / "formulated for African skin" outperforms ingredient lists.
- **Problem-solution specificity**: Lead with the exact problem (dark spots, uneven tone, dull skin) before the solution.
- **Reorder signal as proof**: "Customers reorder every month" is stronger than "loved by customers".
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = problem-led ("Dark spots? Dull skin? This is what's working in Lagos."), Var B = identity ("For Lagos women with melanin-rich skin"), Var C = result ("In 4 weeks, your friends will ask what changed")

❌ Never: "elevate your beauty", "experience the difference", "transform yourself", "feel beautiful"
