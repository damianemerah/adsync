## Universal Hook Taxonomy

Before picking a hook pattern, choose the motivational angle:

| Hook Type       | Formula                             | Nigerian Example                                                   |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Curiosity**   | "I was wrong about [X] until..."    | "I was wrong about wigs. I thought they all shed."                 |
| **Story**       | "Last [week/month], [someone]..."   | "Last Saturday, a customer in Lekki cried when she got her package." |
| **Value**       | "How to [outcome] without [cost]"   | "How to look sharp without breaking the bank"                      |
| **Contrarian**  | "Unpopular opinion: [true thing]"   | "Unpopular opinion: affordable fashion can look premium."          |
| **Urgency**     | "[Number] [people] already [did X]" | "47 orders this week — and it's only Wednesday."                   |
| **Problem**     | "[Pain]? [Empathy statement]."      | "Tired of wigs that shed after one wash? We get it."               |

**Use different hook types across variations** — never write two variations with the same hook type.

### JTBD Framing
People don't buy products. They hire them to do a job.

| Product     | What they're actually hiring it for                                   |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wig         | "Look camera-ready at owambe without 3 hours in a salon"             |
| Ankara gown | "Stand out at the wedding without looking like everyone else"         |
| Bag         | "Carry what I need and still look put-together every day"            |

Frame at least ONE copy variation around the job, not the product.

## FASHION
_Applies to: clothing, bags, shoes, gowns, ankara, aso-ebi, wigs, thrift, accessories_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (≤12 words) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT → LINE 4 PROOF (mandatory) → LINE 5 CTA

### Hook by Tier

| Tier        | Pattern                        | Examples                                                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| low ₦1-10k  | Lead with price/access         | "Lagos fashion from ₦3,500 only 👗" / "Quality bags from ₦5k. Yes, really."               |
| mid ₦10-40k | Lead with desire/social proof  | "The gown Lagos ladies keep coming back for" / "This style sold out twice — we restocked" |
| high ₦40k+  | Lead with exclusivity/scarcity | "Only 6 pieces available." / "Handpicked for the woman who knows what she wants"          |

### Benefit Lines — pick ONE differentiator
- clothing/gowns: event fit | fabric quality | custom sizing | style range
- bags: durability | size/versatility | genuine leather
- wigs: "100% human hair — no shedding, no wahala" | longevity | pre-styled
- Always add location line: "Lagos-based, ships nationwide" / "Same-day delivery within Abuja"

### Proof (mandatory — no fabrication)
✅ Use if stated: exact numbers/ratings
✅ Fallback: "Trusted by Lagos ladies from Island to Mainland" / "Keeps selling out — restock by demand" / "Our customers keep coming back"
❌ Never: fabricated order counts, ratings, or superlatives

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "Send us a message — full collection and prices in minutes" / "DM us. We reply fast."
- Buy now: "Tap to shop — delivery nationwide in 2-3 days"
- Learn more: "Tap to see our full collection and learn more."

### WhatsApp Prefill
"Hi! I saw your ad for [product] in [location]. What styles/sizes do you have, how much, and do you deliver?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Lagos Fashion, Prices You'll Love" | "Quality Ankara. Fast Delivery"
- Urgency: "This Style Keeps Selling Out" | "Only 6 Pieces Left — Don't Wait"

### Psychology Layer
- **Loss aversion (mid/high tier)**: Frame what they'll miss, not what they'll gain. "Lagos women are already asking after this style" outperforms listing features.
- **Identity hook (wigs/beauty-adjacent fashion)**: "Built for the woman who..." resonates stronger than product benefit lists.
- **Scarcity**: Only use when stock is genuinely limited. Never fabricate.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = benefit-led ("100% human hair, no shedding"), Var B = problem-led ("Tired of wigs that shed after one week?"), Var C = social proof ("This is the wig Lagos keeps reordering")

### Pidgin → Copy
e fine well well → premium quality | e cheap → price hook | pepper dem → aspiration | owambe → event-fit benefit | no wahala → convenience

❌ Never: "elevate your wardrobe", "experience fashion differently", "unleash your inner fashionista"
