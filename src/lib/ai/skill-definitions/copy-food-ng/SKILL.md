## Universal Hook Taxonomy

Before picking a hook pattern, choose the motivational angle:

| Hook Type       | Formula                             | Nigerian Example                                                 |
| --------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Curiosity**   | "I was wrong about [X] until..."    | "I was wrong about shawarma. Then I tried this one."            |
| **Story**       | "Last [week/month], [someone]..."   | "Last Saturday, we fed 300 guests and got 14 new bookings."     |
| **Value**       | "How to [outcome] without [cost]"   | "How to serve 100 guests without stressing about food"          |
| **Contrarian**  | "Unpopular opinion: [true thing]"   | "Unpopular opinion: Lagos street food beats most restaurants."  |
| **Urgency**     | "[Number] [people] already [did X]" | "47 orders this week — and it's only Wednesday."                |
| **Problem**     | "[Pain]? [Empathy statement]."      | "Hungry in Lagos at 9pm? We're still delivering."               |

**Use different hook types across variations** — never write two variations with the same hook type.

### JTBD Framing
People don't buy products. They hire them to do a job.

| Product         | What they're actually hiring it for                                    |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Jollof delivery | "Feed 4 people tonight without cooking or leaving the house"           |
| Event caterer   | "Have my guests impressed the moment they walk in — with zero stress"  |
| Cake            | "Make the birthday feel special without spending the whole budget"     |

Frame at least ONE copy variation around the job, not the product.

## FOOD
_Applies to: food, restaurant, catering, cake, shawarma, buka, pastry, chef, small chops, meal prep, event food_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (sensory/occasion) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT → LINE 4 PROOF → LINE 5 CTA

### Hook Patterns
- sensory: "The shawarma Lekki keeps ordering 🔥" / "Jollof that smells like home. Tastes better." / "This pepper soup hits different on a rainy day"
- occasion: "Planning an event in Lagos? Your food problem is solved." / "Birthday, burial, corporate — we cater for all"
- delivery: "Order now. Hot food at your door in [X] mins." / "Hungry in Lagos? We're already on the way."
- price: "A full plate of jollof and chicken for ₦2,500"

### Benefit Lines
- freshness: "Made fresh daily — no reheated, no yesterday's batch" / "Cook-to-order. You taste the difference."
- delivery: "Hot delivery across Lagos — Lekki, VI, Surulere, Yaba. Order by 12pm for same-day."
- caterers: "10 to 1,000 people — birthday, corporate, burial, wedding. Full setup included."
- cakes: "Custom flavour, custom design — baked on order day. No frozen."
- local food: "Homestyle Nigerian cooking — amala, egusi, stew. The real thing."

### Proof
✅ "Lagos customers keep reordering every weekend" / "Popular for corporate lunches and events" / "Fully booked most weekends — order early"
❌ Never: "Nigeria's best shawarma", fabricated order counts

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "WhatsApp us to place your order — confirmed in under 5 minutes" / "DM for full menu, pricing, delivery areas"
- Catering: "Send us your event date and guest count — we quote same day"
- Learn more: "Tap to view our full menu and learn more."

### WhatsApp Prefill
- delivery: "Hi! I'd like to order [food]. Menu, prices, delivery to [area]?"
- caterer: "Hi! Event for ~[X] people on [date]. Can you send menu and pricing?"
- cake: "Hi! Custom cake for [occasion]. Flavours and price for [size]?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Fresh Lagos Food Delivered to You" | "Catering That Makes Events Memorable"
- Urgency: "Book Before This Weekend Fills Up" | "Why Lagos Keeps Ordering This"

### Psychology Layer
- **Sensory specificity beats adjectives**: "pepper soup on a rainy day" > "delicious soup".
- **Occasion urgency (catering)**: "Your event is [X] days away" creates real deadline pressure.
- **Demand signals as proof**: "Fully booked most weekends" signals high demand without fake stats.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = sensory ("The jollof that made her cancel her diet"), Var B = occasion ("Planning an event? We handle the food."), Var C = speed ("Order now. Hot food at your door in 45 mins.")
