## Universal Hook Taxonomy (applies to ALL verticals)

Before picking a hook pattern from the vertical section, choose the motivational angle:

| Hook Type   | Formula                             | Nigerian Example                                           |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Curiosity**   | "I was wrong about [X] until..."    | "I was wrong about wigs. I thought they all shed." |
| **Story**       | "Last [week/month], [someone]..."   | "Last Saturday, a customer in Lekki cried when she got her package." |
| **Value**       | "How to [outcome] without [cost]"   | "How to serve 100 guests without stressing about food" |
| **Contrarian**  | "Unpopular opinion: [true thing]"   | "Unpopular opinion: Lagos street food beats most restaurants." |
| **Urgency**     | "[Number] [people] already [did X]" | "47 orders this week — and it's only Wednesday." |
| **Problem**     | "[Pain]? [Empathy statement]."      | "Tired of wigs that shed after one wash? We get it." |

**Use different hook types across variations** — never write two variations with the same hook type.

### JTBD Framing (Jobs to Be Done — apply at brief stage)
People don't buy products. They hire them to do a job.
Before writing, ask: **"What job is the customer hiring this product for?"**

| Product         | What they're actually hiring it for                                    |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Wig             | "Look camera-ready at owambe without 3 hours in a salon"              |
| Skincare serum  | "Fade dark spots before a big event without harsh chemicals"           |
| Jollof delivery | "Feed 4 people tonight without cooking or leaving the house"           |
| Event decorator | "Have my guests impressed the moment they walk in — with zero stress"  |
| Ankara gown     | "Stand out at the wedding without looking like everyone else"          |
| Electronics     | "Never miss a work deadline because my laptop died at 2pm again"      |

Frame at least ONE copy variation around the job, not the product.

## A. FASHION
_Applies to: clothing, bags, shoes, gowns, ankara, aso-ebi, wigs, thrift, accessories_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (≤12 words) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT → LINE 4 PROOF (mandatory) → LINE 5 CTA

### Hook by Tier

| Tier        | Pattern                        | Examples                                                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| low ₦1-10k  | Lead with price/access         | "Lagos fashion from ₦3,500 only 👗" / "Quality bags from ₦5k. Yes, really."               |
| mid ₦10-40k | Lead with desire/social proof  | "The gown Lagos ladies keep coming back for" / "This style sold out twice — we restocked" |
| high ₦40k+  | Lead with exclusivity/scarcity | "Only 6 pieces available." / "Handpicked for the woman who knows what she wants"          |

### Benefit Lines — pick ONE differentiator
- clothing/gowns: event fit | fabric quality | custom sizing | style range
- bags: durability | size/versatility | genuine leather
- wigs: "100% human hair — no shedding, no wahala" | longevity | pre-styled
- Always add location line: "Lagos-based, ships nationwide" / "Same-day delivery within Abuja"

### Proof (mandatory — no fabrication)
✅ Use if stated: exact numbers/ratings
✅ Fallback: "Trusted by Lagos ladies from Island to Mainland" / "Keeps selling out — restock by demand" / "Our customers keep coming back"
❌ Never: fabricated order counts, ratings, or superlatives

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "Send us a message — full collection and prices in minutes" / "DM us. We reply fast."
- Buy now: "Tap to shop — delivery nationwide in 2-3 days"
- Learn more: "Tap to see our full collection and learn more."

### WhatsApp Prefill
"Hi! I saw your ad for [product] in [location]. What styles/sizes do you have, how much, and do you deliver?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Lagos Fashion, Prices You'll Love" | "Quality Ankara. Fast Delivery"
- Urgency: "This Style Keeps Selling Out" | "Only 6 Pieces Left — Don't Wait"

### Psychology Layer
- **Loss aversion (mid/high tier)**: Frame what they'll miss, not what they'll gain. "Lagos women are already asking after this style" outperforms listing features.
- **Identity hook (wigs/beauty-adjacent fashion)**: "Built for the woman who..." resonates stronger than product benefit lists. Buyers see themselves in the copy first.
- **Scarcity**: Only use when stock is genuinely limited or repeat sell-out demand signals it. Never fabricate.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = benefit-led ("100% human hair, no shedding"), Var B = problem-led ("Tired of wigs that shed after one week?"), Var C = social proof ("This is the wig Lagos keeps reordering")

### Pidgin → Copy
e fine well well → premium quality | e cheap → price hook | pepper dem → aspiration | owambe → event-fit benefit | no wahala → convenience

❌ Never: "elevate your wardrobe", "experience fashion differently", "unleash your inner fashionista"

## B. BEAUTY
_Applies to: skincare, serum, cream, glow, makeup, lash, nail, hair care, natural hair, spa, waxing, organic beauty_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (visible result/identity) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT → LINE 4 PROOF → LINE 5 CTA

### Hook Patterns
- skincare: "The glow your skin has been asking for 💆‍♀️" / "In 4 weeks, your friends will ask what changed" / "Hyperpigmentation, dark spots — let's fix that"
- makeup: "The foundation that matches your skin, not theirs" / "Built for melanin. Finally."
- lash/nail: "Lashes that make people ask 'are those real?'" / "Nails done right — in [loc], by appointment"
- hair products: "Your natural hair IS growing. Wrong products." / "Length retention starts here 🌿"
- organic: "No chemicals. No compromise on results."

### Benefit Lines
- skincare: "Brightening serum for melanin-rich skin — non-greasy, absorbs fast, Nigerian weather tested"
- makeup: "Full coverage — no cracking, no ashiness, sweat-resistant for Lagos heat. Shades from deep brown to ebony."
- lash/nail: service details + appointment flow
- hair: "Seals moisture, prevents breakage — type 4a/4b/4c tested"

### Proof
✅ "Trusted by Lagos women with melanin-rich skin" / "Customers reorder every month" / "Formula built for African skin"
❌ HARD BLOCKS: "lightens skin", "clinically proven" (no citation), "cures acne", "guaranteed to grow", "before/after framing"
❌ Bleaching language → reframe ONLY as: "even tone", "fade dark spots", "brightening"

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "Message us — we'll match you to the right product for your skin type"
- Booking: "Book your appointment — slots fill every week"
- Learn more: "Tap to learn more about our products and services."

### WhatsApp Prefill
- skincare: "Hi! I saw your [serum/cream] ad. I have [concern]. What do you recommend and how much?"
- lash/nail: "Hi! I saw your [service] ad in [loc]. Availability this week and price for [service]?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Skincare Built for Nigerian Skin" | "Your Glow Starts Here"
- Urgency: "Why Lagos Women Keep Reordering This" | "Book Before This Week Fills Up"

### Psychology Layer
- **Identity anchoring**: "Built for melanin" / "formulated for African skin" outperforms ingredient lists. Buyers want to see themselves reflected before they'll read specs.
- **Problem-solution specificity (skincare)**: Lead with the exact problem (dark spots, uneven tone, dull skin) before the solution. Never lead with the product name.
- **Reorder signal as proof**: "Customers reorder every month" is stronger than "loved by customers" — frequency implies proven results.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = problem-led ("Dark spots? Dull skin? This is what's working in Lagos."), Var B = identity ("For Lagos women with melanin-rich skin"), Var C = result ("In 4 weeks, your friends will ask what changed")

❌ Never: "elevate your beauty", "experience the difference", "transform yourself", "feel beautiful"

## C. FOOD
_Applies to: food, restaurant, catering, cake, shawarma, buka, pastry, chef, small chops, meal prep, event food_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (sensory/occasion) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT → LINE 4 PROOF → LINE 5 CTA

### Hook Patterns
- sensory: "The shawarma Lekki keeps ordering 🔥" / "Jollof that smells like home. Tastes better." / "This pepper soup hits different on a rainy day"
- occasion: "Planning an event in Lagos? Your food problem is solved." / "Birthday, burial, corporate — we cater for all"
- delivery: "Order now. Hot food at your door in [X] mins." / "Hungry in Lagos? We're already on the way."
- price: "A full plate of jollof and chicken for ₦2,500"

### Benefit Lines
- freshness: "Made fresh daily — no reheated, no yesterday's batch" / "Cook-to-order. You taste the difference."
- delivery: "Hot delivery across Lagos — Lekki, VI, Surulere, Yaba. Order by 12pm for same-day."
- caterers: "10 to 1,000 people — birthday, corporate, burial, wedding. Full setup included."
- cakes: "Custom flavour, custom design — baked on order day. No frozen."
- local food: "Homestyle Nigerian cooking — amala, egusi, stew. The real thing."

### Proof
✅ "Lagos customers keep reordering every weekend" / "Popular for corporate lunches and events" / "Fully booked most weekends — order early"
❌ Never: "Nigeria's best shawarma", fabricated order counts

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "WhatsApp us to place your order — confirmed in under 5 minutes" / "DM for full menu, pricing, delivery areas"
- Catering: "Send us your event date and guest count — we quote same day"
- Learn more: "Tap to view our full menu and learn more."

### WhatsApp Prefill
- delivery: "Hi! I'd like to order [food]. Menu, prices, delivery to [area]?"
- caterer: "Hi! Event for ~[X] people on [date]. Can you send menu and pricing?"
- cake: "Hi! Custom cake for [occasion]. Flavours and price for [size]?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Fresh Lagos Food Delivered to You" | "Catering That Makes Events Memorable"
- Urgency: "Book Before This Weekend Fills Up" | "Why Lagos Keeps Ordering This"

### Psychology Layer
- **Sensory specificity beats adjectives**: "pepper soup on a rainy day" > "delicious soup". Specific sensory triggers convert better than generic quality claims.
- **Occasion urgency (catering)**: "Your event is [X] days away" creates real deadline pressure without fabricated scarcity.
- **Demand signals as proof**: "Fully booked most weekends" signals high demand without requiring fake stats.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = sensory ("The jollof that made her cancel her diet"), Var B = occasion ("Planning an event? We handle the food."), Var C = speed ("Order now. Hot food at your door in 45 mins.")

### Pidgin → Copy
mama put/buka → homestyle authentic | runs food → delivery speed | e sweet well well → lead with taste | owambe food → event catering | sharp sharp → speed as primary benefit

❌ Never: "culinary experience", "elevate your dining", "gastronomic journey", "farm-to-table"

## D. SERVICES
_Applies to: salon, photographer, event planner, decorator, logistics, repair, clinic, tutor, consultant, agency, cleaning, laundry, mechanic, B2B_

### Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (outcome/problem solved) → LINES 2-3 BENEFIT (reliability + specificity) → LINE 4 PROOF → LINE 5 CTA (sets expectation)

### Hook Patterns

| Service          | Hook angle                                                      |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| salon/stylist    | "Get your hair done by a stylist Lagos keeps rebooking"         |
| photographer     | "Photos that tell your story the way you want it told"          |
| event planner    | "Your event, handled. From setup to takedown."                  |
| logistics        | "Same-day delivery across Lagos — tracked, reliable, on time"   |
| repair/tradesman | "AC not cooling? We fix it today."                              |
| tutoring         | "Your child's grades improve or we work until they do"          |
| agency/B2B       | "We handle your [service] so you can handle your business"      |

### Benefit Lines — reliability + specificity always
- trust: "We confirm every booking. We show up. We deliver." / "Same-day response — no ghosting"
- coverage: "We serve Lekki, VI, Surulere, Yaba, Ikeja and most of Lagos"
- expertise: "Trained in [technique]" / "2 backup cameras — no equipment failure on your day"
- turnaround: "Photos in 48hrs" / "Repairs same-day for most faults" / "Quote in 10 mins on WhatsApp"

### Proof
✅ "Trusted by Lagos clients across events, businesses, homes" / "Repeat bookings are our biggest referral source" / "Schedule fills weeks ahead — book early"
❌ Never: "Lagos's #1 [service]", "100% success rate", "world-class"

### CTA
- WhatsApp: "WhatsApp us — quote same day" / "Send your date and details — confirm availability immediately" / "DM: response under 10 mins during business hours"
- Learn more: "Tap to see our full list of services and learn more."

### WhatsApp Prefill by Type
- salon: "Hi! [service] in [loc]. Availability this week and price for [specific]?"
- photographer: "Hi! Need photographer for [event type] on [date] in [loc]. Packages and pricing?"
- logistics: "Hi! Moving [item] from [area A] to [area B]. Cost and how fast?"
- repair: "Hi! [fault] at my [home/office] in [loc]. When can you come?"

### Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Benefit: "Lagos Services That Actually Show Up" | "Same-Day Delivery Across Lagos"
- Urgency: "Why Lagos Clients Keep Rebooking Us" | "Weekend Slots Filling Fast"

### Psychology Layer
- **Reliability as the primary hook (Lagos context)**: In Lagos, trust is the real purchase driver — more powerful than price or quality claims. Lead with evidence of showing up: "We confirm. We show up. We deliver."
- **Specificity makes claims real**: "Response in 10 mins on WhatsApp" > "fast response". Numbers and specific commitments convert.
- **Retention as social proof**: "Repeat bookings are our biggest referral source" is stronger than generic testimonials — it implies consistent results over time.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = problem-led ("Tired of [service providers] who ghost you?"), Var B = outcome ("Your project/event/home, handled"), Var C = speed ("WhatsApp us. Quote in 10 minutes.")

❌ Never: "elevate your experience", "world-class service", "passion-driven team", "one-stop shop"
