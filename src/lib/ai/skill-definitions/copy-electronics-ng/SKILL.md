# Nigerian Electronics & Tech Copy

## Skeleton
LINE 1 HOOK (performance/value/trust problem) → LINES 2-3 SPEC + REAL-WORLD BENEFIT → LINE 4 TRUST/WARRANTY → LINE 5 CTA

## Hook by Product Type

| Product            | Hook angle                                                                        |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| phones             | "The phone Lagos is talking about — and for good reason"                          |
| laptops            | "Your productivity, upgraded. Finally."                                           |
| gadgets/accessories| "The [product] you didn't know you needed — until now"                            |
| power/inverter     | "No more generator wahala. Seriously."                                            |
| solar              | "Power all day. No fuel. No noise. No bill."                                      |
| sound/audio        | "The sound quality that makes your playlist feel new again"                       |
| used/Tokunbo       | "Grade A condition. UK-used. Verified before it ships."                           |

## Benefit Lines — Spec + Meaning (never spec-dump)
Lead with ONE headline spec, then its real-world outcome:
- "12GB RAM — no freezing when 15 apps are open"
- "6,000mAh battery — full day of Lagos life, one charge"
- "64MP camera — portraits sharp enough to print and frame"
- "Fast charge in under 1 hour — power up on your lunch break"

Add Nigerian context on a second line:
- "Works on all Nigerian networks — MTN, Airtel, Glo, 9mobile"
- "Handles Lagos heat — built for tropical climates"
- "Pay on delivery available within Lagos" (only if stated)

## Trust / Warranty (mandatory for electronics)
This is the primary purchase objection in Nigeria — authenticity anxiety.
✅ "Genuine product — [brand] authorized" / "1-year warranty included" / "Ships sealed, verified authentic"
✅ "Pay on delivery available in [location]" if stated
✅ For used: "Inspected, graded, tested before dispatch. What you see is what you get."
❌ Never claim "original" without verification | Never use "Tokunbo" as a standalone quality claim | Never: "Grade A" without explaining what that means

## CTA
- WhatsApp: "WhatsApp us — specs, photos, and proof of authenticity sent immediately"
- Buy now: "Tap to order — delivery within Lagos in 24hrs"
- Learn more: "Tap to view full specs and confirm availability."

## WhatsApp Prefill
"Hi! I saw your [product] ad. Full specs, price, warranty info — and do you deliver to [area]?"

## Headlines (≤40 chars)
- Spec/value: "128GB. Genuine. Fast Lagos Delivery." | "Your Next Phone Is Here"
- Value: "Premium Tech. Lagos Prices." | "Power Cuts? Not Anymore."
- Trust: "Verified. Warranted. Delivered."

## Psychology Layer
- **Authenticity anxiety is the #1 objection**: Before any feature, buyers want to know it's real. "Genuine product, 1-year warranty" in the first two lines converts better than any spec list.
- **Spec translation is mandatory**: "64MP" alone is noise. "64MP camera — portraits sharp enough to print and frame" is a purchase trigger. Always translate spec → outcome.
- **Price anchoring for high-ticket items**: "Market price: ₦280k. Our price: ₦195k — same product, verified sealed." Anchoring significantly boosts conversion for items ₦50k+.
- **AB variation angles**: Var A = performance ("The phone that keeps up with Lagos"), Var B = value + trust ("₦195k. Genuine. 1-year warranty included."), Var C = problem-led ("Tired of a phone that dies by 2pm or a laptop that freezes mid-presentation?")

❌ Never: "revolutionary technology", "cutting-edge innovation", "next-level performance", "game-changer", "futuristic"
