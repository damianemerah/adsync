# Competitive Battle Cards

Detailed competitive positioning and objection handling for Tenzu vs. major competitors.

## Battle Card Structure

Each competitor analysis includes:
1. **Overview:** What they do, who they serve
2. **Strengths:** What they do well (be honest)
3. **Weaknesses:** Where they fall short
4. **Tenzu Advantages:** Specific differentiators
5. **When to Mention:** Appropriate contexts
6. **Key Messages:** Talking points
7. **Objection Responses:** Common pushbacks

## vs. Wask

### Overview
- **Product:** All-in-one ad management platform (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Google)
- **Target:** US/EU marketers, agencies, e-commerce brands
- **Pricing:** $39-199/month (USD)
- **HQ:** Turkey (US-focused operations)

### Their Strengths
- Mature product (5+ years in market)
- Multi-platform (not just Facebook)
- Large template library
- Desktop experience is polished
- Active community forums

### Their Weaknesses
1. **Payment Friction:** USD payment required, Nigerian cards often fail
2. **Generic Templates:** No AI transformation of user photos
3. **Desktop-First:** Mobile experience is clunky
4. **No Local Focus:** Built for Western markets (no WhatsApp integration, dollar-centric)
5. **Support:** Email-only, slow response for non-US time zones

### Tenzu Advantages
1. **Payment:** Naira via Paystack (zero friction for Nigerian cards)
2. **Creative:** AI redesigns your photos (not just templates)
3. **Mobile-First:** 85% of our users work from phones
4. **WhatsApp-Native:** Built for Nigerian business reality
5. **Local Support:** Nigerian team, WAT time zone, WhatsApp support

### When to Mention Wask
- User mentions payment issues with international tools
- User frustrated with template-only design approach
- User wants mobile-first experience
- User needs WhatsApp integration

### Key Messages

**Payment Angle:**
> "Wask is a great tool—if you have a US credit card. For Nigerian businesses, payment failures are the #1 complaint. Tenzu uses Paystack, so your local card works perfectly."

**Creative Angle:**
> "Wask gives you templates. Tenzu's AI actually redesigns your photos—removes backgrounds, improves lighting, generates lifestyle scenes. Big difference for vendors who shoot products on their phones."

**Mobile Angle:**
> "Wask is built for desktop marketers. Tenzu is built for the 85% of Nigerian users who manage everything from their phone."

### Objection Responses

**"But Wask has more features"**
> "True—Wask does multi-platform ads (TikTok, Google). If you need that, Wask might be better. But if your focus is Facebook/Instagram and you want a tool your Nigerian card actually accepts, Tenzu is built for you."

**"Wask has been around longer"**
> "Absolutely—they've got 5 years on us. But sometimes being newer means we're not carrying old assumptions. We built Tenzu from scratch for Nigerian SMEs. That local focus shows in payment (Paystack), design (AI Studio), and support (Nigerian team in your time zone)."

**"Wask has a bigger community"**
> "Their community is great—mostly US/EU marketers though. Our community is 100% Nigerian SMEs. So when you ask 'Does this targeting work in Lagos?', you're getting answers from people who actually know Lagos."

## Battle Card Usage Guide

### When to Use Battle Cards

**During Sales:**
- User mentions specific competitor
- User comparing tools ("Should I use Wask or Tenzu?")
- User asking "What makes you different?"

**In Content:**
- Blog posts ("Tenzu vs. Wask: Honest Comparison")
- Social proof ("Why I switched from agencies to Tenzu")
- Landing page sections (competitive comparison tables)

**In Support:**
- User frustrated with current tool
- User considering switching
- User asking feature parity questions

### How to Frame Comparisons

**Do:**
- Be honest about competitor strengths
- Focus on specific differentiators (payment, creative, mobile)
- Use "better for" language ("If X matters to you, Y is better")
- Acknowledge use case fit ("For Z type of business, A works well")

**Don't:**
- Trash competitors unfairly ("They suck")
- Make false claims ("We're 100x better")
- Ignore competitor strengths (dishonest)
- Use "one size fits all" messaging ("Perfect for everyone")

### Tone Guidelines

- **Confident but not arrogant:** "We're strong at X" not "We're the best"
- **Specific not generic:** "Paystack integration" not "Better payment"
- **Honest about fit:** "If you need Y, use them" shows integrity
- **Focus on user needs:** What matters to THEM, not what we built
